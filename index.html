Packaging vs distribution diagnostic — click any bucket to see those videos
New viewer growth — core growth signal
NV impressions per video — peaks = growth drivers
Orange peaks = videos that brought in far more new viewers than your average. The multiplier shows how much better they were (e.g. 2.4x = brought in 2.4× the normal amount of new viewers). These are your distribution winners — study what they have in common.
Performance over time
Impressions over time
Rising impressions = YouTube is distributing your content more. If impressions rise but CTR drops, your packaging is the problem. If CTR rises but impressions stay flat, YouTube isn't testing your content widely enough.
CTR over time — NV vs Total
The gap between NV CTR (blue) and Total CTR (dashed) shows how much your loyal audience is inflating your overall CTR. A shrinking gap = your thumbnails are getting stronger for cold audiences.

New vs Returning viewer behaviour
CTR — New vs Returning
When NV CTR is much lower than RV CTR, your title/thumbnail works for fans but not strangers. That's a growth ceiling. You want NV CTR to be competitive — it means cold audiences are also compelled to click.
NVReturning
AVD — New vs Returning (min)
If returning viewers watch much longer, your content rewards familiarity but doesn't hook cold audiences fast enough. Low NV AVD = the first 30 seconds need work.

Subscriber conversion ratios
Sub ÷ View ratio
What % of all viewers subscribe from each video. A spike means that video has unusually strong conversion — find what made it spike and replicate it.
Sub ÷ New Viewer ratio
Of people discovering your channel for the first time, what % subscribe? This is your most important growth metric — it tells you which videos turn strangers into fans.
Subscriber growth — click any bar for full video details
Subs gained per video
Spikes reveal which videos convert viewers into long-term audience members. Cross-reference with Sub/NV ratio — if a video gets subs but low NV%, it's converting your existing audience, not growing it.
Content performance
Performance by topic
High CTR + low retention = title promises more than content delivers. High retention + low CTR = great content, weak packaging. Find topics where both are strong and double down.
CTR by title format
Which title structure (Question, How-To, Statement, Why-Post) consistently gets the most clicks on your channel. Use this to pick your default format for new videos.
Distribution vs packaging — scatter
CTR vs Impressions
Top-right = distribution + packaging both strong. Top-left = good packaging, low distribution. Bottom-right = high reach, weak packaging. Color = performance score.
Retention vs Duration
Shows whether shorter or longer videos hold attention better on your channel. If short videos cluster high on retention, your audience prefers concise content.
