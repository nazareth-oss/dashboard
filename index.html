Performance over time
Impressions over time
Impressions tell you how often YouTube shows your thumbnails. Rising impressions = YouTube is distributing your content more. If impressions rise but CTR drops, your packaging is the problem.
CTR over time (NV vs Total)
NV CTR = how well your title/thumbnail works for people who don't know you. Total CTR includes returning viewers who click regardless. A gap between them reveals how much your audience loyalty is inflating your CTR numbers.

New viewer growth — core growth signal
NV impressions per video — peaks = your growth drivers
This shows how many NEW people each video brought in. Orange peaks = videos that massively outperformed your average for new viewer reach. The multiplier (e.g. 2.4x) tells you how much better that video was at reaching new people vs your typical video. These are your distribution winners.
Distribution vs packaging analysis
CTR vs Impressions
Top right = high distribution + good packaging (winners). Top left = good packaging but low reach (potential hidden gems). Bottom right = high reach but weak packaging. Bottom left = weak on both.
Retention vs Video Duration
Shows whether your shorter or longer videos hold attention better. If short videos cluster high on retention but long ones drop, your audience prefers concise content — or your long-form content needs better pacing.
Content performance by topic & format
Performance by topic — CTR / Views / Retention
Each topic cluster has different CTR and retention patterns. A topic with high CTR but low retention means the title promises something the content doesn't deliver. High retention but low CTR = great content, weak packaging.
Performance by title format
Question titles, How-To, Statement, Why-Post — each has a different CTR profile. Use this to find which format consistently outperforms on your channel, then double down on it.
New vs Returning viewer behaviour
CTR — New vs Returning
When NV CTR is much lower than RV CTR, your title/thumbnail works for fans but not strangers. That's a growth ceiling problem. You want NV CTR to be competitive — it means cold audiences are also compelled to click.
NVReturning
AVD — New vs Returning (minutes)
If returning viewers watch much longer than new viewers, your content rewards familiarity but doesn't hook cold audiences fast enough. The first 30 seconds matter most for NV AVD. Low NV AVD = your hook needs work.

Subscriber conversion
Sub ÷ View ratio per video
What % of all viewers subscribe from each video. A spike means that video has unusually strong CTA alignment, topic relevance, or emotional hook that converts viewers to subscribers. Find what made it spike and replicate it.
Sub ÷ New Viewer ratio per video
This is your cold audience conversion rate. Of everyone discovering your channel for the first time through this video, how many subscribe? This is your most important growth metric — it tells you which videos are best at turning strangers into fans.
